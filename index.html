Food Guide
What to expect at each restaurant
Day 1 · Brunch
Coco Rooftop
Brunch · 200–280 MAD pp
A generous Moroccan spread-style brunch — msemen flatbreads, honey, amlou, fresh juices, eggs and pastries. Light, colourful and perfect after a long morning of travel and sightseeing.
Day 1 · Dinner
La Pergola & Le Bistro Arabe
Dinner · 400–500 MAD pp · Live jazz from 7:30pm
Franco-Moroccan fusion in an elegant riad setting. Refined takes on Moroccan classics — slow-cooked lamb, beautifully spiced tagines — alongside French bistro dishes. Cocktails and live jazz make it feel celebratory.
Day 2 · Lunch
Dar Cherifa
Lunch · 200–350 MAD pp · Cash only
Simple, homestyle Moroccan in one of the most atmospheric spaces in the medina. Short menu — salads, soups, tagines, bastilla — but everything made with care. The food is secondary to the experience of eating in a 16th-century candlelit courtyard.
Day 2 · Dinner
Le Slimana Rooftop
Dinner · 250–400 MAD pp · Wine available
Modern Moroccan-Mediterranean fusion — creative, beautifully plated. The caramelised octopus is a standout, the lamb ravioli blends French technique with Moroccan spicing, and the pistachio crème brûlée is a lovely East-meets-West finish. A step above the typical medina rooftop.
Day 3 · Lunch
La Terrasse du Jardin
Lunch · 200–280 MAD pp
Honest, hearty traditional Moroccan. Rfissa is a rich dish of shredded chicken, lentils and fenugreek over msemen — deeply comforting. Kofta tagine brings spiced minced lamb in tomato-egg sauce. The Moroccan crepes with honey and butter are irresistible for dessert.
Day 3 · Dinner (you cook it!)
Lalla Zehra Farm
Cooking class + dinner · 600 MAD all-inclusive
You'll make a classic Moroccan tagine of your choice (chicken with preserved lemon & olives, or lamb with prunes & almonds) and zaalouk — a smoky, silky aubergine and tomato dip. Everything cooked over charcoal in traditional clay pots. Farm-fresh, Berber family recipes. One of the most memorable meals of the trip.
Day 4 · Lunch (included in tour)
Home-cooked Berber lunch — Atlas Mountains
Included in tour price · vegetarian available
The most authentic eating of the whole trip. A Berber family spread: harira soup, fresh salads dressed with cumin and lemon, a slow-cooked mountain tagine, couscous with seven vegetables, and seasonal fruit. Simple, nourishing, made from whatever's in the valley that day.
Day 4 · Dinner
Folk Marrakech
Dinner · 250–350 MAD pp · Gnawa show from 8:30pm
Contemporary Moroccan with a fun, buzzy atmosphere drawing on Berber, Arab and Andalusian influences — pastilla, tagines, couscous — presented with a modern edge. The food plays second fiddle to the gnawa music and belly dancing, but it's genuinely good. Cocktails flow freely.
Day 5 · Lunch
Café des Épices
Lunch · 80–150 MAD pp · No reservation needed
Relaxed and simple — exactly what you want on a slow day. Light Moroccan café food: fresh salads, kefta brochettes, briouats (crispy parcels filled with cheese or kofta), tagines. Nothing complicated, everything fresh. The rooftop setting over the spice square is half the meal.
Day 5 · Dinner
Le Jardin
Dinner · 200–350 MAD pp · Wine available
Beautiful open-air courtyard garden restaurant balancing traditional Moroccan with Mediterranean influences. Tagines are the heart of the menu — tender, aromatic, slow-cooked. Salads are fresh and colourful. The setting under the trees at night is genuinely magical, making it the perfect wind-down dinner.
Day 6 · Farewell lunch
Café Dar L'Hssira
Lunch · 50–100 MAD pp · Cash only
Pure, unfussy Moroccan home cooking at its best. Harira to start — the classic tomato, lentil and chickpea soup with a squeeze of lemon. Then tagines, couscous, or pastilla (the showstopper pigeon or chicken pie dusted with icing sugar and cinnamon). A proper send-off that tastes like Morocco itself.
🌿 The thread through every meal: slow-cooked, aromatic, spiced with warmth rather than heat — cumin, coriander, saffron, preserved lemon, ras el hanout. You'll eat extraordinarily well.
